Core:

Identity and Values

Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now

The hardest ethical decision I have ever had to make was leaving Russia and moving to New Zealand. My husband and I used to take part in the protests trying to fight for free elections, but after the annexation of Crimea we just gave up. 85% of the country supported this horrifying act of aggression that was the biggest step leading to the present war. We felt helpless and silenced. We had to weigh our hope for a brighter future for us and our hypothetical children against our love for our families that would stay behind. I am still not sure if it was the right decision because we paid for it dearly, but our parents always supported us and wanted us to have a safe life outside of Russia. There is also an implication that you are choosing your own freedom and comfort over staying and trying to dismantle the system and promote change.

Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

My parents have definitely shaped my values and identity. While many of the values we shared were very important, for example compassion, humour, risk-taking and lifelong desire to try new things, one trumps them all. It's this innate appreciation for freedom. Freedom in general, freedom of speech (my parents were journalists), freedom of choice... That appreciation was not reflected in the society I lived in. Whether I like it or not, I was still brought up on Russian culture, literature and art. I am Russian. To say that I'm having an identity crisis now that my homeland invaded Ukraine would be an understatement. I would very much rather identify as a kiwi, but I don't feel like I belong here yet although I finally think of Christchurch as home.

Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

I believe that my biggest strengths are my curiosity and a somewhat creative side. I love learning new stuff, so it organically keeps me excited throughout my journey. I try to be open to different ideas and point of views, and I'm not afraid to change my beliefs. I also value humour and try a lighter approach when dealing with interpersonal drama.

Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

I am naturally very introverted and reserved and it takes a while for me to reveal my true sarcastic self to others. I may come off as arrogant or clinically shy until I work through my initial anxiety. I am trying to consciously go out of my way to be more social and connect with people during this experience because we just don't have any time for wallowing. Secondly, I am a bit of a lone wolf. I'm used to working on my own and working quickly — I've been a solo freelancer since I was 17 with only a year-long stint in an office (big yikes, not a fan). So the prospect of team assignments, pair programming and being patient with others scares the living crap out of me.

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

The latest example of some resistance when working/studying together was during my intro to programming class at Ara, but it was mainly due to the fact that I couldn't connect to what I perceived as a "boys' club". Reflecting back, it wasn't my gender, but my age and, to be totally transparent, lack of enthusiasm, that stopped me from finding common ground with young people straight out of school. I didn't use any real strategies and just carried on, but looking forward I believe I should be more vocal about my ideas and try to be more engaging with my peers — there's always some common interests, passions or experiences that can bring us closer. No need for me to sulk in the corner.
