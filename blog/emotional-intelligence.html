Emotional Intelligence

What is emotional intelligence?

Emotional intelligence is an awareness of not only your own emotions, but also the emotions of others. People with higher emotional intelligence can reflect on their emotions and control them while also empathising with others.

How is it different from IQ?

IQ, or cognitive intelligence in a traditional sense, focuses on reasoning, logic, processing information and memory. EQ, on the other hand, is about mastering social interactions, assessing emotions and reigning them in.

Why is it important?

Emotional intelligence is very important when you are trying to have meaningful interactions with other people. It lets you easily put yourself in someone else's shoes and better relate to their experience. Being in tune with your own emotions helps you grow as a person and become more content with your life. Recognizing your emotions, for example, anger or frustration, can help you pause and "feel your feelings" instead of acting on them.

Moreover, individuals with high EI are shown to achieve great academic and workplace results, especially in leadership positions.
