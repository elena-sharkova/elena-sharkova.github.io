Learning Plan: Bootcamp

Long term goals

I hope to work on something meaningful and practical and not just consumerism-focused. Ideally, it's a start-up in Christchurch! üòä

Strengths and limitations

One of my limitations I've re-discovered during Foundations is that I struggle to ask for help because it feels like cheating. If I can't Google my way out of it or find relevant discussions on Discord, I could spend hours trying to solve a problem by myself. It does feel more rewarding in the end and I tend to remember the issue better, but I don't think it's going to be viable during Bootcamp.

Non-technical challenges

I think of one of the biggest non-technical challenge during Bootcamp is going to be routine and working on my own after I'm done for the day. I fear it is going to be so draining that I would want to switch off, but I will have to push through and study in the evenings as well. The other massive challenge is definitely going to be pair programming. I am very introverted by nature and I need to recharge after long conversations. I am absolutely horrified that I will have to spend all day on video/audio calls and have little to no downtime at all. It's also pretty scary in terms of producing working code because how am I going to think while talking to my pair?

Humans skills to work on

I would like to work on my collaboration and communication skills, especially in group settings. I don't want to be too quiet during discussions nor silence the ideas of others. I believe that working in pairs even leading to our very first group assignment is going to help immensely.¬†

Expectations from the Bootcamp team

I hope to get more detailed technical feedback from the Bootcamp team than during Foundations, hopefully with some code reviews. I expect the team to guide us through at least the first pair programming and team assignments. A good way of showing support would be acknowledging that we are doing something really difficult and emotionally (and even somewhat physically) demanding.

Expectations from myself

If I'm struggling emotionally, I will reach out to my loved ones, my peers or the Human Skills team depending on the issue. For technical problems, I plan to ask my peers/tutors more questions if things don't quite click. In terms of managing my workload, I hope I'll be able to stick to my timetable/planner and avoid working late into the night. I should really keep using the Toggl because Pomodoro timers provide me with external motivation. I can be very irritable if I miss out on sleep, and my peers and tutors don't deserve to experience that. üòä My mental health is going to be extra important during Bootcamp, so I will focus on techniques that help me bring my stress levels down.

My biggest strengths are probably my curiosity and a desire to get my hands dirty, so to speak. I struggle with big chunks of theoretical information without any practice. My limitations are connected to a trait I actually like about myself, my pace. I like to work quickly, I try and research topics as fast as I can, but my impatience is often my downfall. Some things you just can't rush.
