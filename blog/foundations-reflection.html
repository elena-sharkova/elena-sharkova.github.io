Core:

Foundations Reflection

What are the three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

It may sound arrogant, but I feel like I know myself pretty well at this point in my life, so there wasn't necessarily a lot of self-discovery happening during Core. However, there were parts of my personality that I had to re-examine for this whole intense experience and it felt quite useful.

Let's start with one of my not-so-appealing traits — I am very competitive. During the first couple of weeks it was really hard to let go of trying to gauge how everyone else was doing and just focus on myself. Naturally, I still struggle with that sometimes, but I am much more worried about my own technical ability at this point. Ego? What ego? Trying to cram all of this new information into my poor brain is very humbling. So, for now if someone is doing great, I draw inspiration and try to learn new tricks from them, if someone is having difficulties, I strive to help if I can.

Once again I had to face my introversion, but I was deliberately trying to go out of my way to reach out and be more open. While I'm never going to be the life of the party, I've managed to make some connections with awesome like-minded people. And honestly, to me it seems as important as all of the technical knowledge I'm gaining.

I really enjoyed helping (or at least trying to!) others with their issues. It's a very satisfying feeling when you get through a problem and it finally works, and you've shared a bit of knowledge with someone else. I can sometimes be a grumpy "have you ever heard of Google" kind of gal, so it does feel great when I overcome that.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Programming is very collaborative, and it's not this super-exclusive club that only a few chosen ones have access to, so you will meet a lot of new people. Everyone is going to have their own view of life, values and challenges you may have never even considered before. It's essential to treat your colleagues or peers with respect and empathy, regardless of your own views. It does feel weird to say it because it's what you do in every other aspect of your life. Be kind to others, don't be judgemental, expand your horizons and be self-aware. Reflect on your behaviour if you feel like you've been rude to someone, don't just shrug it off.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

I was quite surprised by the looping exercise. We had to pair up and listen to the other person talk about an issue for 2 minutes straight without giving any verbal feedback (that's so difficult!). It felt very rewarding to just completely focus on the other person and actually listen to what they are saying. And same goes the other way, it felt great to be heard!

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

I found the very first blog entry the most challenging. Talking about your identity, values and ethical decisions in your very first exercise? That seemed very overwhelming and it feels like I could've done better with this bit a few weeks in. On the other hand, it set the pace and depth for further self-reflection.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

To teach us how to best learn a lot of new information quickly (something that will apply throughout our programming careers), how to take care of yourself during that process and how to collaborate and communicate with others.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

I don't think it was a waste of time, even though I usually took much longer than suggested timeboxes to work through my answers (and I'm doing it again right now). The Core part really complements the technical learning and helps rewire your brain and shift your attitude. When you employ some of the techniques in good faith, they really help to view inevitable frustration and pain of learning in a completely different light — you are just growing!
